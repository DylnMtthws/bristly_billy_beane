<scoring_rubric>
- 10: Format-defining for this strategy; near-mandatory inclusion
- 8-9: Strong fit; significantly improves the deck's plan
- 6-7: Solid fit; supports the strategy
- 4-5: Reasonable inclusion but interchangeable with alternatives
- 2-3: Weak fit; doesn't directly support the strategy
- 1: Anti-synergy; actively works against the strategy
</scoring_rubric>

<evaluation_guidance>
IMPORTANT: The commander's strategic profile may describe "value inversions" —
cases where normally-weak card characteristics become strengths. If the profile
lists value inversions, evaluate cards against the INVERTED value framework,
not generic heuristics. A 0/4 defender creature that would normally score 2-3
could score 8-9 if the commander turns defenders into attackers.

When the profile specifies desired card characteristics, prioritize cards that
match those characteristics over generically "good" cards.

ENGINE vs. OUTPUT: When the profile lists "engine_dependencies," the commander's
strategy has a causal chain: ENGINE → OUTPUT. A card that produces the commander's
OUTPUT (e.g., life drain) without feeding the ENGINE (e.g., Aura count) is a
FALSE SYNERGY. Score it on general utility (3-5), NOT on output similarity (7-9).
Only cards that feed the engine or directly amplify the engine's outputs deserve
high synergy scores.

Example: For an Aura-drain commander, a lifelink creature scores 3-4 (doesn't
feed the Aura engine). An Aura that enchants opponents' creatures scores 8-9
(feeds the engine AND produces the output).

CALIBRATION: Use the full 1-10 range. If the profile lists "mispriced cards,"
those represent 7-9 quality for this specific commander. Cards similar to the
mispriced examples (same traits, same engine interaction) should also score 7-9.
Generic good-stuff with no commander-specific value should score 4-5, not 6-7.

Avoid score clustering. If most cards score 5-6, you are not differentiating
enough. For a value-inversion commander, the scoring distribution should be
bimodal: cards that match the inverted framework score 7-9, cards that don't
score 3-5.
</evaluation_guidance>

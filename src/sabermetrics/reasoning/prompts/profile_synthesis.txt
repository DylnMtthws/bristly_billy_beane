<task>
Synthesize these evidence streams into a strategic profile. Your reasoning must:

1. **Prioritize card text** as the primary source of truth for what the commander mechanically does. Inclusion data is corroboration, not authority.

2. **Note where evidence streams diverge.** If players consistently include cards the text doesn't obviously suggest, that signals discovered synergy worth highlighting.

3. **Identify multiple build paths**, ranked by community support:
   - Mainstream consensus (high inclusion + community confirmation)
   - Emerging strategies (some inclusion + community discussion)
   - Text-supported but underexplored paths

4. **Surface anti-synergies** — popular cards that don't actually fit, with reasoning.

5. **Estimate power level range** using the WotC bracket framework (1-5), not a single number.

6. **Cite specific rules** when they affect your reasoning (e.g., "CR 702.2 confirms flash creatures count as cast for trigger purposes").

7. **Use rulings** when they clarify edge-case interactions relevant to the commander's strategy.

8. **Identify value inversions** — If this commander transforms normally-weak
   card characteristics into strengths (e.g., defender creatures becoming
   attackers, high-CMC spells becoming cheap, sacrifice becoming an upside),
   articulate the inversion explicitly: what characteristics become valuable,
   why, and what card selection criteria follow from it. Include a
   "desired_card_characteristics" list specifying the traits to look for.

9. **Map engine dependencies** — Identify the commander's core mechanical
   engine (the thing the deck must accumulate or activate) and distinguish
   it from the engine's DEPENDENT OUTPUTS (effects the engine produces as
   consequences). Cards that replicate an output independently without
   feeding the engine are FALSE SYNERGIES — they look aligned but don't
   actually advance the game plan.

   For each engine dependency, specify:
   - engine: what the deck must build around
   - engine_card_traits: oracle text patterns / card types that feed the engine
   - dependent_outputs: effects the engine produces
   - false_synergy_warning: why cards matching outputs but not engine are traps

   Example: Eriette's engine is "Auras you control." The outputs are "life
   drain" and "creature lockdown." A lifelink creature drains life but doesn't
   increase Aura count — it's a false synergy. A cheap Aura that enchants an
   opponent's creature is a true synergy — it feeds the engine AND produces
   the output.

10. **Identify mispriced cards** — Name 5-10 specific cards that satisfy ALL of:
    a. Legal in this commander's color identity
    b. Would score poorly under generic Magic heuristics (bad stats, niche type,
       overcosted without the commander, etc.)
    c. Are excellent specifically for THIS commander's strategy

    For each card, provide:
    - card_name: exact Scryfall card name
    - why_undervalued: one sentence explaining why generic analysis misses its value

    Examples of what qualifies:
    - Wall of Denial for Arcades (0/8 defender = 8-damage flyer for 3 mana)
    - Dead Weight for Eriette (bad removal becomes Aura that feeds drain engine)
    - Flamewave Invoker for Agatha ({8} ability becomes {1} with power 7+)

    If the commander does NOT invert conventional heuristics (e.g., a tribal
    lord that just wants the best creatures of its type), explicitly set
    mispriced_card_examples to an empty array. Do not force mispriced cards
    that don't exist.
</task>
